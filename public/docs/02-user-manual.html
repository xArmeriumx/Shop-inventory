1. Getting Started

1.1 Creating an Account

  1. Open the application URL in your browser.
  2. Click Register on the login page.
  3. Fill in your name, email address, and password.
  4. Click Create Account.

1.2 Setting Up Your Shop

After your first login, the system will guide you through the onboarding process.

  1. Enter your shop name. This will appear on receipts and in the system header.
  2. Add your shop's address, phone number, and optionally a tax ID.
  3. Set an invoice prefix (default: INV). This prefixes all your sale invoice numbers.
  4. Click Create Shop to complete setup.

Once the shop is created, you become the Owner with full access to all features.

1.3 Logging In

  1. Enter your email and password on the login page.
  2. Click Sign In.
  3. You will be redirected to the Dashboard.
Note

If you are a staff member (not the owner), your shop owner must invite you first via Settings > Team Management.

2. Dashboard

The dashboard is the first page you see after logging in. It shows a summary of your shop's performance for the selected date range.

2.1 Summary Cards

CardWhat It Shows
RevenueTotal net sales amount (after discounts) for active sales in the period.
ExpensesTotal recorded expenses in the period.
ProfitRevenue minus total cost of goods sold.
Sales CountNumber of completed sales transactions.

2.2 Notifications

The bell icon in the header shows unread notifications. Common notifications include:

  • Low Stock: A product's stock has fallen below its minimum threshold.
  • New Sale: A sale was completed (useful for owners tracking staff activity).

3. Products

3.1 Adding a Product

  1. Navigate to Products from the sidebar.
  2. Click Add Product.
  3. Fill in the required fields:
FieldRequiredDescription
NameYesProduct display name.
SKUYesUnique product code. Must be unique within your shop.
Sale PriceYesThe price customers pay.
Cost PriceYesThe price you paid to acquire this product. Used for profit calculations.
StockYesCurrent on-hand quantity.
Min StockNoLow-stock alert threshold. A notification is triggered when stock falls below this number.
CategoryNoProduct category (configurable in Settings > Lookups).
ImageNoProduct photo. Uploaded to cloud storage.
DescriptionNoInternal notes about the product.

3.2 Editing a Product

  1. Click on any product row in the product list.
  2. Modify the desired fields.
  3. Click Save.
Concurrent Edits

If another user edits the same product at the same time, the system will show a "Product has been modified by another user" error. Reload the page and try again.

3.3 Adjusting Stock

To manually adjust stock without creating a sale or purchase:

  1. Open the product detail page.
  2. Click Adjust Stock.
  3. Enter the adjustment amount (positive to add, negative to subtract).
  4. Provide a reason for the adjustment.
  5. Click Confirm.

The adjustment is logged in the stock audit trail with the reason you provided.

3.4 CSV Import

  1. Click Import on the products page.
  2. Download the CSV template to see the expected format.
  3. Fill in your product data in the template.
  4. Upload the completed CSV file.
  5. Review the preview and click Import.

3.5 Deleting a Product

Deleting a product is a soft delete. The product is hidden from the catalog but remains in the database for historical reference (past sales will still show the product name).

4. Point of Sale (Creating a Sale)

4.1 Starting a New Sale

  1. Navigate to Sales > New Sale or use the POS page.
  2. Search or browse products to add them to the cart.
  3. Adjust quantities using the +/- buttons or by typing directly.

4.2 Applying Discounts

Item-Level Discount

  1. Click the discount icon on any line item in the cart.
  2. Enter a discount percentage (e.g., 10 for 10% off).
  3. The item subtotal updates immediately.

Bill-Level Discount

  1. Look for the Discount section below the cart summary.
  2. Choose Percentage or Fixed Amount.
  3. Enter the value. The net total updates in real time.

4.3 Selecting Payment Method

MethodBehavior
CashPayment is immediately marked as verified.
TransferPayment starts as Pending. The customer or staff uploads a payment slip image. A staff member with "Payment Verify" permission must approve it.
CreditSame as Transfer — starts as Pending and requires verification.

4.4 Choosing a Sales Channel

Select the channel where this sale originated: Walk-in, Shopee, Lazada, LINE, Facebook, or Other. This helps with per-channel reporting.

4.5 Assigning a Customer

Optionally search for and select an existing customer, or create a new one inline. Linking a customer allows you to track their purchase history.

4.6 Confirming the Sale

  1. Review the cart, discounts, total, and payment method.
  2. Click Confirm Sale.
  3. The system generates an invoice number, deducts stock, and saves the transaction.
  4. A receipt is displayed that can be printed or downloaded.

4.7 Printing a Receipt

After confirming a sale, click Print Receipt to send to an 80mm thermal printer. The receipt includes: shop name, address, invoice number, date, itemized products, discounts, total, payment method, and a thank-you message.

5. Managing Sales

5.1 Viewing Sales

Navigate to Sales to see a paginated list of all sales. Use the search bar and date range filter to find specific transactions.

5.2 Sale Detail

Click on any sale row to see the full detail: items, quantities, discounts, total, payment status, customer, and any associated shipments or returns.

5.3 Payment Verification

For sales paid by Transfer or Credit:

  1. Open the sale detail page.
  2. The payment slip image (if uploaded) is visible.
  3. Click Approve or Reject.

Requires the PAYMENT_VERIFY permission.

5.4 Cancelling a Sale

  1. Open the sale detail page.
  2. Click Cancel Sale.
  3. Enter a cancellation reason.
  4. Click Confirm.

Cancellation restores all stock, cancels any associated shipments, and marks the sale as CANCELLED. The record is preserved for audit purposes.

6. Purchases

6.1 Recording a Purchase

  1. Navigate to Purchases > New Purchase.
  2. Select the Supplier from the dropdown (or create a new one).
  3. Add products and set the quantity and cost price per unit.
  4. Select the payment method (Cash, Transfer, or Credit).
  5. Optionally add notes.
  6. Click Confirm Purchase.

On confirmation:

  • Stock for each product is increased by the purchased quantity.
  • Each product's cost price is updated to the new purchase cost.
  • A purchase number (PUR-00001) is generated.

6.2 Cancelling a Purchase

  1. Open the purchase detail page.
  2. Click Cancel Purchase.
  3. Enter a cancellation reason.

The system will:

  • Check that sufficient stock exists to reverse the purchase (if the stock has been sold, cancellation is blocked).
  • Decrease stock by the originally purchased quantity.
  • Revert each product's cost price to the previous purchase cost.

7. Shipments

7.1 Creating a Shipment

  1. Open the sale detail page for a sale that needs shipping.
  2. Click Create Shipment.
  3. Fill in the recipient name, shipping address, shipping provider, tracking number, and shipping cost.
  4. Click Create.

The shipment starts with Pending status.

7.2 Updating Status

7.3 Shipping Cost

When a shipping cost is entered, the system can automatically create a corresponding expense record if the shipment is linked to an expense category.

8. Returns

8.1 Processing a Return

  1. Open the original sale's detail page.
  2. Click Create Return.
  3. For each item to be returned, enter the return quantity.
  4. The system calculates the refund amount automatically, accounting for any discounts applied to the original sale.
  5. Enter a reason for the return.
  6. Click Confirm Return.

8.2 What Happens on Return

  • Returned quantities are added back to stock.
  • The original sale's net amount and profit are reduced by the refund amount.
  • The item's returned quantity counter is incremented (prevents returning more than was sold).
  • A return number (RET-00001) is generated.
Partial Returns

You can return some items from a sale without returning all. Multiple returns can be created against the same sale, as long as the total returned quantity per item does not exceed the originally sold quantity.

9. Customers

9.1 Adding a Customer

  1. Navigate to Customers > Add Customer.
  2. Enter name, phone, email, and any notes.
  3. Click Save.

9.2 Managing Addresses

Each customer can have multiple saved shipping addresses. These addresses are used when creating shipments.

  1. Open the customer detail page.
  2. Click Add Address.
  3. Enter the recipient name and full address.
  4. Optionally mark an address as default.

9.3 Customer Profile

The customer profile page shows aggregated statistics: total purchases, total spending, number of returns. The purchase history lists all linked sales.

10. Suppliers

10.1 Adding a Supplier

  1. Navigate to Suppliers > Add Supplier.
  2. Enter the supplier code (unique), name, contact person, phone, email, and tax ID.
  3. Click Save.

10.2 Supplier Profile

View all purchases linked to the supplier, total spending, and contact details.

11. Expenses and Income

11.1 Recording an Expense

  1. Navigate to Expenses > Add Expense.
  2. Select a category (configurable via Settings > Lookups).
  3. Enter the amount and optional description.
  4. Set the date.
  5. Click Save.

11.2 Recording Non-Sale Income

For income that is not from a sale (e.g., freelance work, refund received):

  1. Navigate to Income > Add Income.
  2. Enter the details and click Save.

11.3 Effect on Dashboard

Expenses reduce the Net Profit displayed on the dashboard. Income entries are tracked separately from sales revenue.

12. Reports and Export

12.1 Available Reports

Reports can be exported to CSV or Excel format. Click the Export button on any report page and select the desired format.

13. Settings

13.1 Shop Profile

Update your shop's name, address, phone, tax ID, and invoice prefix. These appear on receipts and documents.

13.2 Team Management

As the shop owner, you can invite staff members and assign them roles.

Inviting a Team Member

  1. Navigate to Settings > Team.
  2. Click Add Member.
  3. Enter the staff member's email address.
  4. Select a role from the available roles.
  5. Click Invite.

The invited user must already have an account. Once added, they can log in and access features according to their assigned role.

Changing a Member's Role

  1. Open the team member list.
  2. Click on the member you want to modify.
  3. Select a new role from the dropdown.
  4. Click Save.

13.3 Role Management

  1. Navigate to Settings > Roles.
  2. Click Add Role to create a new role.
  3. Name the role (e.g., "Cashier", "Warehouse").
  4. Select permissions from the checklist. Each permission corresponds to a specific action in the system.
  5. Click Save.

13.4 Lookup Values

Lookup values are configurable dropdown options used throughout the system:

Lookup TypeUsed In
Product CategoriesProduct form — categorization
Expense CategoriesExpense form — type of expense
Income CategoriesIncome form — type of income

Add, edit, or remove lookup values via Settings > Lookups.

14. AI Receipt Scanner

14.1 How It Works

  1. Navigate to Purchases > New Purchase.
  2. Click the Scan Receipt button.
  3. Upload a photo of the supplier's receipt or invoice.
  4. The system extracts text from the image using OCR (Optical Character Recognition).
  5. An AI model analyzes the extracted text and identifies product names, quantities, and unit costs.
  6. The extracted data is pre-filled into the purchase form.
  7. Review the extracted data, make corrections if needed, and confirm the purchase.
Accuracy Note

OCR accuracy depends on the quality of the receipt photo. Clear, well-lit photos with legible text produce the best results. Always review the extracted data before confirming.
